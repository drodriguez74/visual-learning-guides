Risk Assessment

Fintech-Specific Concerns

Autonomous AI engineers in regulated environments require deliberate guardrails. These are the failure modes that matter at Fiserv scale.

High Risk
Data Residency & PII Leakage
Devin sessions may process production-like data. Ensure all task inputs are sanitized. Never pass live cardholder data or PII to the agent context — synthetic data only during development tasks.
High Risk
Prompt Injection via Codebase
Malicious instructions embedded in code comments, README files, or Jira descriptions can redirect the agent. All task inputs should be validated before reaching the agent.
Medium Risk
Over-Automation of Review Gates
Devin can open PRs that look thorough but contain subtle logic errors in financial calculations. Your two-approval MR policy is the right control — never auto-merge Devin PRs.
Medium Risk
SOX Change Management Gaps
Any Devin-generated change touching financial reporting systems must go through your standard change management process. Devin does not replace ITSM ticketing for regulated systems.
Managed Risk
Non-Deterministic Output
Devin may produce different implementations on repeated runs. For critical paths, enforce output determinism via strict spec and snapshot tests — not just functional tests.
Opportunity
Audit Completeness Advantage
Devin's session replay log is actually a compliance asset — every decision is attributable and replayable. This exceeds what a human developer's git history provides for SOX audit purposes.
