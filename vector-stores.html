How language becomes geometry — and why proximity means meaning

§ 04 How Similarity Is Measured
Cosine
cos(θ) = A·B / |A||B|
Measures the angle between two vectors, ignoring magnitude. Two vectors pointing the same direction = similarity of 1. Opposite = −1.
★ Best for NLP / embeddings
Euclidean
√Σ(aᵢ − bᵢ)²
Straight-line distance in space. Sensitive to magnitude — longer vectors appear further even if direction matches.
★ Good for image embeddings
Dot Product
Σ aᵢ · bᵢ
Magnitude × alignment. Faster to compute than cosine (no normalization). Used in retrieval systems optimized for speed at scale.
★ Used in FAISS / ANN index
